# Market Sizing

This sizing is intentionally lightweight and judge-friendly. Where the numbers are inferences, they are labeled as such.

## Problem anchors

- UN Tourism reported 1.4 billion international tourist arrivals in 2024.
  Source: https://www.unwto.org/news/international-tourism-recovers-pre-pandemic-levels-in-2024
- WHO states that medication errors are associated with an estimated USD 42 billion in annual global cost.
  Source: https://www.who.int/initiatives/medication-without-harm
- WHO states that about 1 in 10 patients experience harm in health care and around 50% of that harm is preventable.
  Source: https://www.who.int/news/item/18-04-2024-who-launches-first-ever-patient-safety-rights-charter
- CDC analysis published in 2025 found that more than 194 million US adults reported at least one chronic condition in 2023. We use this only as proxy evidence that a large share of adult travelers carry medically relevant context.
  Source: https://www.cdc.gov/pcd/issues/2025/24_0539.htm

## TAM framing

### Traveler base

- Base global traveler population for the problem: 1.4 billion annual international arrivals.

### Medically relevant traveler segment

- Conservative inference: if even 10% of those trips involve travelers with high-value emergency context such as allergies, anticoagulants, implants, epilepsy, diabetes, or recent discharge information, that is roughly 140 million relevant traveler journeys per year.

### Initial commercial buyer set

- Hospitals and urgent care networks in tourism-heavy corridors
- Travel insurers and medical assistance desks
- Corporate mobility and study-abroad programs

## Narrow wedge

MedAgent does not need to win general records interoperability first. The wedge is:

- travelers with high-risk medication or allergy context
- care encounters where a clinician needs a compact emergency subset fast
- organizations that need auditable access without sharing full records

## Why the market is credible

- Travel volume is already very large.
- Unsafe care and medication error remain expensive and common.
- The wedge avoids the hardest integration problem: full longitudinal records exchange.
- The product can be sold as a narrow operational risk-reduction tool before it grows into broader access workflows.
